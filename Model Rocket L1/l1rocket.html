Level 1 Model Rocket


Tufts Rocketry Club, 2023 - 2024


A high-powered rocket was built to achieve level 1 certification, capable of securely reaching an altitude of 2728 feet with an H-class motor. The fin assembly was made out of laser-cut birch wood, the nosecone was 3D printed, and a cardboard body tube was used, all simulated in OpenRocket and modeled in OnShape. The rocket flew on April 6, 2024 only to be lost in a cloud. Due to it going missing, an identical rocket was made using new techniques. It flew on April 21, 2024 where it ascended smoothly off the path, performed a stable ascent, deployed the parachute at 300 feet, and returned safely to ground.

The fin assembly was laser cut into centering rings and 4 fins, with the top and bottom centering rings cut out of 1/4" birch plywood and the rest out of 1/8" birch. They were then epoxied together and sanded to a smooth finish. To prepare the body tube, fin slots were cut using a table saw and the fin assembly was later epoxied into the slots. A parachute was installed, the nosecone was fitted, the motor was fit-checked, and the rocket was painted.
