Bottle Rockets


Tufts CEEO Perez Lab, 2024


A bottle rocket activity was designed to introduce middle school students to aerospace engineering along with aerodynamic and physics concepts. The activity consisted of exposing students to momentum, moment of inertia, and center of mass through live demonstrations followed by an explanation of the different parts of a bottle rocket: nosecone, fins, parachute, and body. Finally, students were able to build their bottle rockets using cardboard, a parachute, and a 2-liter soda bottle and launch them.

The activity was tested several times in English and Spanish with diverse student groups. The launcher was made out of PVC pipes, a bike pump, and zip ties and was tested with example bottle rockets. To launch the rockets, a combination of water and air was used. The demonstrations included throwing a ball while in a chair with wheels, balancing a pencil with one finger, and a ball falling into a cup.
